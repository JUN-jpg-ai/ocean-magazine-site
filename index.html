Special Report:
The State of
Our Oceans

Beneath the surface lies a world in flux—warming currents, shifting migrations, and ecosystems balancing on a razor’s edge. This year’s survey reveals both crisis and quiet hope.

From the coral nurseries of the Great Barrier Reef to the deep-sea trenches being mapped for the first time, scientists are racing to document what may soon be lost.

Satellite data shows that phytoplankton blooms, the very lungs of our planet, have shifted by hundreds of miles in the last decade alone.

Yet in the depths, there are still places where nature’s grandeur remains untouched—where giants still roam.
